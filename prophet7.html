Prophet Muhammad

The Life of Prophet Muhammad

Prophet Muhammad (Peace Be Upon Him) was born in Mecca in 570 CE. He was known as Al-Amin, the trustworthy, even before receiving his prophethood. At the age of 40, he began receiving revelations from Allah through the angel Jibril (Gabriel), which continued for 23 years. These revelations were compiled into the Quran, the holy book of Islam.

His Mission

Prophet Muhammad's mission was to call people to the worship of the One God, Allah, and to lead them to righteousness and justice. He emphasized social equity, kindness, and morality. His teachings stressed the importance of community, charity, and helping those in need.

Key Events

Prophet Muhammad faced many challenges, including persecution, battles, and the migration (Hijrah) to Medina, where he established the first Islamic state. He also led the famous Battle of Badr, which resulted in a decisive victory for the Muslims. After years of struggle, he peacefully conquered Mecca, where he forgave his enemies and cleansed the Kaaba of idols.

Legacy

The legacy of Prophet Muhammad is profound. His teachings continue to guide over 1.8 billion Muslims worldwide. He is the final prophet, and his life is considered a perfect example of integrity, patience, and dedication to Allah’s message. His Sunnah (traditions) continues to inspire and influence the daily lives of Muslims.

The Final Revelation

The Quran, revealed to Prophet Muhammad over 23 years, remains the final and unchanged word of Allah. It is a complete guide for humanity, covering all aspects of life. Prophet Muhammad’s life and actions exemplified the teachings of the Quran, making him the ultimate role model for Muslims around the world.
